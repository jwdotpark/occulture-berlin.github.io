The Art of Transforming The World - Tarot and the Future of Immersive Narrative
Adam Malone
Storytelling today is becoming increasingly challenged by the extraordinary potential of immersive technology. This technological leap demands a paradigm shift in narrative expression and calls upon us to discover new models and approaches to story creation that will allow us to match the potential of these new and exciting technological mediums. One possible source of inspiration for future storytellers could lie with the commonly misunderstood ‘art’ of Tarot, a ‘meaningful card game’ that whilst standing upon some key modes and rules of expression allows the potential for a deeper awareness of ourselves as central protagonist in the ‘story of who we are’. Reasons as to how and why Tarot is able to make such claims have remained largely housed within the Western Esoteric traditions for the last 250 years. However, Tarot could now stand ready to emerge as an influence upon our future stories in a way that even the most adept fortune-tellers may not have been able to predict!
Adam Malone is currently working as a freelance Immersive Event Producer for Phenomen Films in London. He runs Club Imaginal in Brighton, a regular lecture series as well as The Medicine Shed in Forest Row, working with the healing traditions of indigenous cultures. His current MA thesis for the Myth, Cosmology and The Sacred programme at Christchurch University, Canterbury is entitled “Transforming The World : Tarot and the Future of Immersive Narrative”. In the past he has completed a BA/BSc in Film, Media and Cultural Studies, having majored in Avant Garde Film and Video and now spends his spare time experimenting with live immersive cinema.
